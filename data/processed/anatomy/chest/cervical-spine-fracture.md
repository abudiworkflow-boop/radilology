**Cervical spine fractures** can occur secondary to exaggerated flexion or extension, or because of direct trauma or axial loading.

#### Epidemiology

Males are affected more commonly than females with a median age of injury of 56 years. Falls, [motor vehicle collisions](/articles/missing?article%5Btitle%5D=motor-vehicle-collision&lang=us), pedestrian accidents, cycling and diving are common causes of injury 6,7. 

#### Pathology

The [cervical spine](/articles/cervical-spine?lang=us) is susceptible to injury because it is highly mobile with relatively small [vertebral bodies](/articles/missing?article%5Btitle%5D=vertebral-bodies&lang=us) and supports the head which is both heavy and acts as a lever. C2 (~30%) and C7 (~20%) are the most commonly fractured levels 7.

There are many types of cervical spine fracture, some of which are unstable; general indicators of instability include 9:

- increased or reduced [intervertebral disc space height](/articles/missing?article%5Btitle%5D=intervertebral-disc-space-height&lang=us)

- increased [interspinous distance](/articles/missing?article%5Btitle%5D=interspinous-distance&lang=us)

- [facet joint widening](/articles/missing?article%5Btitle%5D=facet-joint-widening&lang=us)

- [vertebral compression](/articles/spinal-compression-fracture?lang=us) >25%

- more than one [vertebral column](/articles/three-column-concept-of-spinal-fractures?lang=us) involvement ref required

PediatricThe fulcrum of movement is different in children than adults, C2/3 compared to C5/6, respectively; hence, in children, cervical fractures are more common in upper vertebrae. There are also other anatomical differences of the cervical spine between children and adults which are worth bearing in mind while interpreting pediatric studies. These include more horizontal orientation of the facet joints in children, underdeveloped uncovertebral joints, mild physiological anterior wedging of the vertebral bodies, and incomplete ossification of the odontoid process.

AssociationsAssociated injuries are present in ~67% of patients 7:

- [blunt cerebrovascular injury (BCVI)](/articles/blunt-cerebrovascular-injury?lang=us) especially in high cervical fractures, those associated with subluxation and fractures involving the transverse foramen

- [rib](/articles/rib-fractures?lang=us) and/or [sternal fractures](/articles/sternal-fracture?lang=us)
