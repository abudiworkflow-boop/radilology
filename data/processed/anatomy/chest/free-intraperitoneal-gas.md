**Pneumoperitoneum** (aeroperitoneum is a rare synonym 12) describes gas within the [peritoneal cavity](/articles/peritoneal-cavity?lang=us), often due to critical illness. There are numerous causes and several [mimics](/articles/pseudopneumoperitoneum?lang=us).

#### Pathology

The most common cause of pneumoperitoneum is the disruption of the wall of a hollow viscus. In children, the causes are different from the adult population and are considered in the [neonatal pneumoperitoneum](/articles/neonatal-pneumoperitoneum?lang=us) article.

The causes and, hence, the corresponding severity of accompanying illness, are variable:

- 
perforated hollow viscus

[peptic ulcer disease](/articles/peptic-ulcer-disease?lang=us)

- [ischemic bowel](/articles/mesenteric-ischaemia?lang=us)

- [bowel obstruction](/articles/bowel-obstruction?lang=us)

- [necrotizing enterocolitis](/articles/necrotizing-enterocolitis?lang=us)

- [appendicitis](/articles/acute-appendicitis-2?lang=us)

- [diverticulitis](/articles/colonic-diverticulitis-1?lang=us)

- malignancy

- [inflammatory bowel disease](/articles/inflammatory-bowel-disease-ibd?lang=us)

- 
mechanical perforation

[trauma](/articles/trauma?lang=us)

- colonoscopy

- [foreign bodies](/articles/foreign-body-1?lang=us) 

- iatrogenic

- [postoperative free intraperitoneal gas](/articles/postoperative-free-intraperitoneal-gas?lang=us)

- [peritoneal dialysis](/articles/missing?article%5Btitle%5D=peritoneal-dialysis&lang=us)

- 
vaginal "aspiration" 3

cunnilingus

- douching

- sudden squatting

- postpartum exercises

- water-skiing 10

- mechanical ventilation

- [pneumomediastinum](/articles/pneumomediastinum?lang=us)

- [pneumothorax](/articles/pneumothorax?lang=us)

#### Radiographic features

Plain radiographChest radiographAn erect chest x-ray is probably the most sensitive plain radiograph for the detection of free intraperitoneal gas as it can detect minimal amount of gas (~1 mL) under the diaphragm 14.

If a large volume pneumoperitoneum is present, it may be superimposed over a normally aerated [lung](/articles/lung?lang=us) with normal lung markings.

- [subdiaphragmatic free gas](/articles/subdiaphragmatic-free-gas?lang=us), often forming a lucent crescent ([crescent sign](/articles/crescent-sign-disambiguation?lang=us))

- [leaping dolphin sign](/articles/leaping-dolphin-sign?lang=us)

- [cupola sign](/articles/cupola-sign-1?lang=us) (on supine film)

- [continuous diaphragm sign](/articles/continuous-diaphragm-sign?lang=us)

Abdominal radiographFree gas within the peritoneal cavity can be detected on an abdominal radiograph. The signs created by the free intraperitoneal air can be further divided by anatomical compartments in relation to the pneumoperitoneum:

- 
bowel-related signs

​​[double wall sign](/articles/rigler-sign-bowel?lang=us) (also known as [Rigler sign](/articles/rigler-sign-bowel?lang=us) or bas-relief sign)

- [telltale triangle sign](/articles/telltale-triangle-sign?lang=us) (also known as the triangle sign or telltale triangle) 

- 
peritoneal ligament-related signs

[football sign](/articles/football-sign-pneumoperitoneum?lang=us)

- [falciform ligament sign](/articles/falciform-ligament-sign-1?lang=us)

- [lateral umbilical ligament sign](/articles/inverted-v-sign-pneumoperitoneum?lang=us) (also known as [inverted "V" sign](/articles/inverted-v-sign-pneumoperitoneum?lang=us))

- [urachus sign](/articles/urachus-sign?lang=us)

- 
right upper quadrant signs

[cupola sign](/articles/cupola-sign-1?lang=us)

- [fissure for ligamentum teres sign](/articles/fissure-for-ligamentum-teres-sign?lang=us)

- [hepatic edge sign](/articles/hepatic-edge-sign?lang=us)

- [lucent liver sign](/articles/lucent-liver-sign?lang=us)

- [Morison's pouch sign](/articles/doge-cap-sign-pneumoperitoneum?lang=us) ([doge cap sign](/articles/doge-cap-sign-pneumoperitoneum?lang=us))

- [periportal free gas sign](/articles/periportal-free-gas-sign?lang=us)

UltrasoundMaybe useful in the appropriate clinical setting. A [linear-array](/articles/linear-array?lang=us) transducer (10-12 MHz) is considered more sensitive than a standard [curvilinear](/articles/convex-array?lang=us) abdominal transducer (2-5 MHz).

Recognized direct features include:

- 
enhancement of the peritoneal stripe ([peritoneal stripe sign](/articles/peritoneal-stripe-sign-pneumoperitoneum?lang=us)) 7

either alone or associated with repeating, horizontal long-path reverberation artifacts which extend into the far field

- 
discrete, hyperechoic foci representing gas bubbles

may adopt a linear arrangement, often associated with short path reverberation artifacts which appear as [comet tails](/articles/comet-tail-artifact-4?lang=us), or if tapering, [ring down](/articles/ring-down-artifact-1?lang=us) artifacts

- may also cluster in a manner that results in the attenuation and diffuse reflection of reflected ultrasound waves, which results in an underlying inhomogenous (or dirty) acoustic shadow

Ultrasonography may also be combined with dynamic maneuvers

- free intraperitoneal air is expected to demonstrate movement with patient position

- it may also be displaced with caudal pressure on the probe above the collection, reappearing with cessation of the pressure

- unlike air within the lung, does not demonstrate respirophasic changes

Using contrast-specific software generally used during [contrast-enhanced ultrasound](/articles/contrast-enhanced-ultrasound-2?lang=us) (CEUS) can substantially accentuate free abdominal air, thereby increasing the chance of detection 13. 

#### Differential diagnosis

- consider [pseudopneumoperitoneum](/articles/pseudopneumoperitoneum?lang=us)
