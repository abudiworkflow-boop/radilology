**Spinal cord compression (SCC)** is a surgical emergency, usually requiring prompt surgical decompression to prevent permanent neurological impairment. If the spinal roots below the [conus medullaris](/articles/conus-medullaris?lang=us) are involved, and there are characteristic symptoms and signs, it is termed [cauda equina syndrome](/articles/cauda-equina-syndrome?lang=us).

#### Pathology

EtiologyThere are numerous causes of cord compression. These can be divided according to the location of the compressing mass:

[intervertebral disc](/articles/intervertebral-disc?lang=us)

[disc protrusion](/articles/disc-protrusion?lang=us)/[disc extrusion](/articles/disc-extrusion?lang=us)

- [discitis osteomyelitis](/articles/spondylodiscitis?lang=us) (usually associated with an epidural abscess)

- degenerative anterolisthesis/[spondylosis](/articles/spondylosis?lang=us)

- 
vertebral

trauma

[vertebral fracture](/articles/spinal-fractures?lang=us) (e.g. [burst fracture](/articles/burst-fracture?lang=us))

- fracture-dislocation

- 
tumor

[vertebral metastasis](/articles/vertebral-metastases?lang=us)

- [primary vertebral tumor](/articles/missing?article%5Btitle%5D=primary-vertebral-tumour&lang=us) (e.g. [aggressive vertebral hemangioma](/articles/aggressive-vertebral-haemangioma?lang=us))

- iatrogenic

- cement leakage following [vertebroplasty or kyphoplasty](/articles/vertebroplasty-and-kyphoplasty?lang=us)

- 
epidural space

[epidural abscess](/articles/spinal-epidural-abscess?lang=us) or [phlegmon](/articles/phlegmon-1?lang=us)

- [epidural hematoma](/articles/spinal-epidural-hematoma-2?lang=us)

- [epidural metastasis](/articles/spinal-metastasis-1?lang=us) 

- [epidural hydatid cyst](/articles/hydatid-disease?lang=us)

- 
dura

[spinal meningioma](/articles/spinal-meningioma?lang=us)

- [spinal arachnoid cyst](/articles/spinal-arachnoid-cyst?lang=us)

- [ventral herniation of the cord](/articles/ventral-herniation-of-the-cord?lang=us)

- 
intradural space

[nerve sheath tumor](/articles/spinal-nerve-sheath-tumours-1?lang=us) ([spinal schwannoma](/articles/spinal-schwannoma?lang=us) or [neurofibroma](/articles/neurofibroma?lang=us))

Lesions of the cord itself can present in a similar manner to extrinsic cord compression but are usually considered separately (e.g. [spinal cord tumors](/articles/missing?article%5Btitle%5D=spinal-cord-tumours&lang=us), [spinal cord abscess](/articles/spinal-cord-abscess?lang=us)).

- [epidural spinal cord compression scale](/articles/epidural-spinal-cord-compression-scale?lang=us)
