                Spondylolistheses
              

          

        
      
  

    
    
    

    
  

    
    
    

    
          
**Spondylolisthesis** (plural: spondylolistheses) denotes the slippage of one [vertebra](/articles/vertebra?lang=us) relative to the one below.

#### Terminology

Although etymologically, it is directionless (see below) and could be applied to both anterolisthesis and retrolisthesis, in practice, spondylolisthesis is used synonymously with [anterolisthesis](/articles/anterolisthesis?lang=us). Often, particularly in the lower lumbar spine, it is due to [spondylolysis](/articles/spondylolysis?lang=us) ([pars interarticularis](/articles/pars-interarticularis?lang=us) defects) 6. 

#### Epidemiology

The incidence of spondylolisthesis in childhood is ~5%, mostly due to [spondylolysis](/articles/spondylolysis?lang=us). Incidence increases in adulthood to ~12.5% (range 10-15%), mostly due to degenerative change 9.

#### Clinical presentation

When symptomatic, patients present with [low back pain](/articles/low-back-pain?lang=us), [radiculopathy](/articles/missing?article%5Btitle%5D=radiculopathy&lang=us) and/or [neurogenic claudication](/articles/missing?article%5Btitle%5D=neurogenic+claudication&lang=us) 9.

#### Pathology

Spondylolisthesis can occur anywhere in the vertebral column but is most frequent in the lumbar spine, particularly when due to [spondylolysis](/articles/spondylolysis?lang=us) at L5/S1 9, and when due to degeneration at L4/5 7. 

#### Classification

- [Wiltse classification](/articles/wiltse-classification-of-spondylolisthesis?lang=us): divides into types by etiology

- [Meyerding classification](/articles/meyerding-classification-of-spondylolisthesis?lang=us): grades by severity of the slip

#### Radiology report

To adequately describe a spondylolisthesis, the type and grade must be stated ref and the presence or absence of instability 4.

#### Radiographic features

Plain radiographOn lateral [lumbar spine x-rays](/articles/lumbar-spine-appa-view?lang=us), a "step" in the alignment of the posterior cortices can be visible and then graded by the Meyerding classification. The type of spondylolisthesis may also be able to determined. On [flexion/extension views](/articles/lumbar-spine-flexion-and-extension-views?lang=us), translation >4 mm indicates instability 4.

#### Treatment and prognosis

Indications for surgical management of spondlylolisthesis includes 9:

- symptomatic grade 1/2 slips refractory to non-operative management (usually 6 months)

- grade 3 or higher slips

- progressive slip > 1 grade

- instability

#### History and etymology

Spondylolisthesis is from the Ancient Greek σπονδύλους (spondylous) meaning vertebra and ολίσθηση (olisthesis) meaning slippage 6. **Carl von Rokitansky, **(1804-1878), an Austrian pathologist, was the first to describe spondylolisthesis in 1839 8.
