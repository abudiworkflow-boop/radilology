#### Terminology

- [consolidation](https://radiopaedia.org/articles/consolidation?lang=us) is often erroneously misinterpreted as a synonym for pneumonia

- some [interstitial lung diseases](https://radiopaedia.org/articles/interstitial-lung-disease?lang=us) have been classified as interstitial pneumonia; some would argue that it would be less confusing to use the term pneumonitis for these inflammatory/fibrotic diseases

#### Epidemiology

Pneumonia ranks 8th as a cause of death in the USA and is the most deadly of the infectious diseases. Poverty, age, and access to vaccination and antibiotics are the main causes of global variation. In 2019, 2.5 million people died of ‘clinical’ pneumonia, and pneumonia was the leading cause of death for children under 5. The [COVID-19 pandemic](https://radiopaedia.org/articles/covid-19-4?lang=us "COVID-19") is responsible for over 7 million deaths 3.

#### Classification

Pneumonias can be classified by:

- etiology
  - infective agent
    - [bacterial (pyogenic) pneumonia](https://radiopaedia.org/articles/bacterial-pneumonia?lang=us)
      - [cavitating bacterial pneumonia](https://radiopaedia.org/articles/cavitating-pneumonia?lang=us)
    - [fungal pneumonia](https://radiopaedia.org/articles/pulmonary-fungal-disease?lang=us)
      - [pneumocystis pneumonia (PCP)](https://radiopaedia.org/articles/pulmonary-pneumocystis-jirovecii-infection-2?lang=us)
    - [mycobacterial pneumonia](https://radiopaedia.org/articles/missing?article%5Btitle%5D=mycobacterial-pneumonia&lang=us)

    - [viral pneumonia](https://radiopaedia.org/articles/viral-respiratory-tract-infection-1?lang=us)
      - [coronavirus](https://radiopaedia.org/articles/human-coronavirus-1?lang=us)
        - [COVID-19](https://radiopaedia.org/articles/covid-19-4?lang=us)

        - [Middle East respiratory syndrome (MERS) infection](https://radiopaedia.org/articles/middle-east-respiratory-syndrome-coronavirus-mers-cov-infection?lang=us)

        - [severe acute respiratory syndrome (SARS)](https://radiopaedia.org/articles/severe-acute-respiratory-syndrome-1?lang=us)
      - [varicella pneumonia](https://radiopaedia.org/articles/varicella-pneumonia?lang=us)
  - setting of infection
    - [community-acquired pneumonia](https://radiopaedia.org/articles/community-acquired-pneumonia?lang=us)

    - [hospital-acquired pneumonia (HAP)](https://radiopaedia.org/articles/hospital-acquired-pneumonia-hap?lang=us)

    - [ventilator-associated pneumonia (VAP)](https://radiopaedia.org/articles/ventilator-associated-pneumonia-2?lang=us)
  - lipid: [lipoid pneumonia](https://radiopaedia.org/articles/lipoid-pneumonia?lang=us)

  - [aspiration pneumonia](https://radiopaedia.org/articles/aspiration-pneumonia?lang=us "Aspiration pneumonia")
- method of spread (a pathological description)
  - [bronchopneumonia](https://radiopaedia.org/articles/bronchopneumonia?lang=us)

  - [lobar pneumonia](https://radiopaedia.org/articles/lobar-pneumonia?lang=us), single or [multilobar pneumonia](https://radiopaedia.org/articles/multilobar-pneumonia?lang=us)

  - interstitial pneumonia
- radiographic appearance
  - [atypical pneumonia](https://radiopaedia.org/articles/atypical-pneumonia?lang=us)

  - [round pneumonia](https://radiopaedia.org/articles/round-pneumonia-1?lang=us)

  - [cavitating pneumonia](https://radiopaedia.org/articles/cavitating-pneumonia?lang=us)

  - [hemorrhagic pneumonia](https://radiopaedia.org/articles/haemorrhagic-pneumonia?lang=us)

#### History and etymology

The word ‘pneumonia’ is derived from the Greek pneumon and literally means lung disease ref.

Although pneumonia has been recognized for millennia, the cause was a mystery until the 1880s when _Streptococcus pneumoniae_ was first identified ref.

#### Practical points

- 60% of community-acquired pneumonia is diagnosed clinically without identification of a causative organism or imaging studies ref
