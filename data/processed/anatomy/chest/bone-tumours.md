The term **bone tumor** or **bone lesion** can be applied to a bewildering number of entities including primary and metastatic neoplasms as well as a variety of metabolic, developmental, lymphoid, reactive and tumor-like lesions that affect bone1-4. Hence, there are a wide variety of radiological appearances.

A systematic approach taking into account location, patient demographic and imaging appearances can often substantially narrow the differential. This is especially important if the mass is a benign ["don't touch" lesion](https://radiopaedia.org/articles/leave-alone-lesions-skeletal?lang=us "Do not touch bony lesions") that could be safely ignored or observed.

This is an overview article; for more information please refer to the articles on each subtype described below.

#### Epidemiology

Different tumor types have different epidemiological manifestations. Primary bone tumors are rare and are vastly outnumbered by [metastases](https://radiopaedia.org/articles/bone-metastases-1?lang=us "Metastases to bone") and hematopoietic tumors. The true incidence of primary bone tumors is difficult to determine due to their rarity and because most benign bone tumors are asymptomatic and go undetected 2,8,9.

Of the primary malignant bone cancers, the most common are [osteosarcoma](https://radiopaedia.org/articles/osteosarcoma?lang=us "Osteosarcoma") (35%), [chondrosarcoma](https://radiopaedia.org/articles/chondrosarcoma?lang=us "Chondrosarcoma") (30%), and [Ewing sarcoma](https://radiopaedia.org/articles/ewing-sarcoma?lang=us "Ewing sarcoma") (16%) 5. These have different age distributions but there is a slight male predominance with a ratio of 1.3:1.

#### Clinical presentation

Benign bone tumors are often asymptomatic and identified incidentally. Others, however, can produce pain, cause a slow-growing mass, or result in a [pathologic fracture](https://radiopaedia.org/articles/pathological-fracture?lang=us "Pathologic fracture") 8.

#### Pathology

Most primary bone lesions are benign solitary phenomena, with a propensity for the long bones of the extremities 8. The presence of multiple bone lesions should raise suspicion for other entities, with metastases and multiple myeloma being the most common osteolytic lesions in patients older than 30 years 1,3.

There are numerous individual tumor entities which come under the umbrella term "bone tumor". For a formal and updated classification of bone tumors, see [WHO classification of tumors of bone](https://radiopaedia.org/articles/who-classification-of-tumours-of-bone?lang=us "WHO classification of tumors of bone"). #### Radiographic features

The radiographic appearance of the lesion provides essential information in differentiating bone tumors, with patient age being the most important clinical clue. The most reliable indicator in determining whether a lesion is aggressive (typically malignant) or nonaggressive (typically benign) is the zone of transition between the lesion and the adjacent normal bone 3.

Please see the [describing a bone lesion](https://radiopaedia.org/articles/describing-a-bone-lesion?lang=us "Describing a bone lesion") and [aggressive versus non-aggressive bone lesion](https://radiopaedia.org/articles/aggressive-vs-non-aggressive-bone-lesions-radiographs?lang=us "Aggressive versus nonaggressive bone lesions") articles for more detail.

##### Plain radiograph

Radiography is the optimal initial imaging modality for evaluating undiagnosed primary bone tumors 1-3,7. The radiographic features of a bone lesion are often the primary determinant of non-histological diagnosis.

Essential features to describe when assessing a potentially neoplastic bone lesion include:

- location in the skeleton ( [flat](https://radiopaedia.org/articles/flat-bones?lang=us "Flat bones") vs [long](https://radiopaedia.org/articles/long-bones?lang=us "Long bones") bones or [axial](https://radiopaedia.org/articles/axial-skeleton?lang=us "Axial skeleton") vs [appendicular](https://radiopaedia.org/articles/appendicular-skeleton?lang=us "Appendicular skeleton"))

- location within a bone
  - longitudinal: [epiphyseal](https://radiopaedia.org/articles/epiphysis?lang=us "Epiphysis"), [metaphyseal](https://radiopaedia.org/articles/metaphysis?lang=us "Metaphysis") or [diaphyseal](https://radiopaedia.org/articles/diaphysis?lang=us "Diaphysis")

  - transverse: [medullary](https://radiopaedia.org/articles/bone-marrow?lang=us "Bone marrow"), eccentric, [endosteal](https://radiopaedia.org/articles/endosteal-scalloping?lang=us "Endosteal scalloping"), cortical, or periosteal
- [zone of transition](https://radiopaedia.org/articles/transition-zone-disambiguation?lang=us "Zone of transition (disambiguation)")

- [matrix](https://radiopaedia.org/articles/matrix-bone?lang=us "Matrix (bone)")

- morphology

- [periosteal reaction](https://radiopaedia.org/articles/periosteal-reaction?lang=us "Periosteal reaction")

- size

- cortical involvement

- extra-osseous or soft-tissue component

##### CT/MRI

Advanced imaging modalities, such as CT, MRI, or nuclear medicine play a complimentary role when additional anatomic information is required for surgical planning and staging, or when clinical or radiographic features are indeterminate 2.

CT can enhance the ability to detect subtle [matrix mineralization](https://radiopaedia.org/articles/matrix-bone?lang=us "Matrix (bone)") or [periosteal reaction](https://radiopaedia.org/articles/periosteal-reaction?lang=us "Periosteal reaction") that may not be seen on radiographs or MRI.

MRI provides added soft-tissue contrast, which allows for evaluation of the tissue composition (e.g. fat, hemorrhage, fluid-fluid levels) and anatomic extent of bone tumors.

#### Treatment and prognosis

Treatment and prognosis vary not only with stage but also with histologic type.

More indolent processes may be managed with curettage combined with local adjuvants such as bone cement (polymethyl methacrylate), bone graft, phenol or liquid nitrogen.

In general, for more aggressive lesions, surgery, chemotherapy, and radiotherapy are offered according to the [stage](https://radiopaedia.org/articles/tnm-staging-system-2?lang=us "TNM staging system"), resectability, operability, and functional status. Targeted treatments depend on molecular testing.
