#### Epidemiology

Sarcoidosis occurs all over the world in all ages and races. Attempts to describe accurate epidemiology are complicated by the use of inconsistent diagnostic criteria and variable (often asymptomatic) 9 disease manifestations.

Still, certain epidemiological patterns are reported in the literature:

- age of onset
  - most commonly presents between 2nd through 4th decades of life, although diagnosis in children and elderly also recognized 3
- gender
  - inconsistent data 6

  - there may be a small female predominance among African-Americans 3,6
- race
  - the highest incidence in African-Americans (36 to 50 per 100,000) 5 and northern European Caucasians

  - incidence among Caucasians has been estimated at 11 to 20 per 100,000 4,5

  - the reported incidence is lower in Asian populations
- heritability
  - familial clustering of sarcoidosis has been reported, suggesting either a genetic or environmental component of the disease 3,5,7

  - the two largest studies suggest a familial relative risk increase ~4x given a single affected first-degree relative 7

#### Diagnosis

The American Thoracic Society recognizes (2020) three major diagnostic criteria 14:

- compatible clinical presentation

- non-necrotizing granulomatous inflammation in one or more tissue samples

- exclusion of alternative causes of granulomatous disease

The World Association of Sarcoidosis and Other Granulomatous Disorders (WASOG) has also published detailed organ-specific criteria (2014) 15.

#### Clinical presentation

Clinical presentation is highly variable depending on the organ systems affected. Approximately half of all patients are asymptomatic. In symptomatic patients, the disease can take relapsing or progressive courses if left untreated. In these symptomatic patients, the most commonly involved organ systems are [respiratory](https://radiopaedia.org/articles/sarcoidosis-thoracic-manifestations-2?embed_domain=buyreviewer.com%25252525252Fbest-tire-inflator-on-amazon%25252525252F&lang=us) (e.g. cough and dyspnea) and dermatological (e.g. [erythema nodosum](https://radiopaedia.org/articles/erythema-nodosum?embed_domain=buyreviewer.com%25252525252Fbest-tire-inflator-on-amazon%25252525252F&lang=us), lupus pernio, scars, plaques) systems 3. Ocular, lacrimal gland and salivary gland, and cardiac involvement are also relatively common. Approximately 5% of patients develop [neurosarcoidosis](https://radiopaedia.org/articles/neurosarcoidosis?embed_domain=buyreviewer.com%25252525252Fbest-tire-inflator-on-amazon%25252525252F&lang=us) 4, but this is rarely a presenting clinical phenotype.

Additionally, patients may have specific syndromic presentations:

- [Löfgren syndrome](https://radiopaedia.org/articles/l-fgren-syndrome?embed_domain=buyreviewer.com%25252525252Fbest-tire-inflator-on-amazon%25252525252F&lang=us): an uncommon but specific acute presentation of sarcoidosis with a better prognosis

- [Heerfordt syndrome](https://radiopaedia.org/articles/heerfordt-syndrome?embed_domain=buyreviewer.com%25252525252Fbest-tire-inflator-on-amazon%25252525252F&lang=us): an uncommon variant of sarcoidosis comprising of [fever](https://radiopaedia.org/articles/pyrexia?embed_domain=buyreviewer.com%25252525252Fbest-tire-inflator-on-amazon%25252525252F&lang=us), [parotid enlargement](https://radiopaedia.org/articles/parotid-gland-enlargement-1?embed_domain=buyreviewer.com%25252525252Fbest-tire-inflator-on-amazon%25252525252F&lang=us), facial palsy, and anterior uveitis

#### Pathology

Sarcoidosis is a multisystem disorder of unknown etiology characterized by the formation of inflammatory non-caseating granulomas within affected tissues. Histologically, the lesions characteristically demonstrate an absence of a necrotic component, except in rare cases (so-called " [necrotizing sarcoid granulomatosis](https://radiopaedia.org/articles/necrotising-sarcoid-granulomatosis?embed_domain=buyreviewer.com%25252525252Fbest-tire-inflator-on-amazon%25252525252F&lang=us "Necrotising sarcoid granulomatosis")"). The [granulomas](https://radiopaedia.org/articles/granuloma?embed_domain=buyreviewer.com%25252525252Fbest-tire-inflator-on-amazon%25252525252F&lang=us "Granulomas") may resolve spontaneously or progress to fibrosis.

It is thought to represent a disorder of immune regulation, particularly of cell-mediated immunity 3. Mycobacterium and propionibacterium RNA and DNA have been detected in sarcoidosis lesions, raising the possibility of an infectious trigger 4. Reported familial clustering and co-occurrence in monozygotic twins suggest that genetic susceptibility may also play an important role.

##### Microscopic appearance

Characteristic (but not [pathognomonic](https://radiopaedia.org/articles/pathognomonic?embed_domain=buyreviewer.com%25252525252Fbest-tire-inflator-on-amazon%25252525252F&lang=us)) histological features include:

- non-caseating granulomas 5,14
  - epithelioid histiocytes, macrophages, multinucleated giant cells, and CD4+ T cells

  - fibroblasts, B lymphocytes, and CD8+ T cells may be present more peripherally
- [Schaumann bodies](https://radiopaedia.org/articles/missing?article%5Btitle%5D=schaumann-bodies&embed_domain=buyreviewer.com%25252525252Fbest-tire-inflator-on-amazon%25252525252F&lang=us): laminated calcium-containing concretions

- [asteroid bodies](https://radiopaedia.org/articles/missing?article%5Btitle%5D=asteroid-bodies&embed_domain=buyreviewer.com%25252525252Fbest-tire-inflator-on-amazon%25252525252F&lang=us)

- necrosis is atypical and may suggest another etiology 5

##### Markers

Biochemical markers include:

- elevated [angiotensin-converting enzyme (ACE)](https://radiopaedia.org/articles/angiotensin-converting-enzyme-ace?embed_domain=buyreviewer.com%25252525252Fbest-tire-inflator-on-amazon%25252525252F&lang=us)
  - 40% false negative

  - 10% false positive 3
- hypercalcemia and hypercalciuria

Additionally, a positive [Kveim-](https://radiopaedia.org/articles/missing?article%5Btitle%5D=kveimstilzbach-skin-test&embed_domain=buyreviewer.com%25252525252Fbest-tire-inflator-on-amazon%25252525252F&lang=us) [Siltzbach skin](https://radiopaedia.org/articles/missing?article%5Btitle%5D=kveim-siltzbach-skin&embed_domain=buyreviewer.com%25252525252Fbest-tire-inflator-on-amazon%25252525252F&lang=us) test helps to confirm the diagnosis 3.

#### Radiographic features

90% of patients have pulmonary involvement (although many are asymptomatic) 8. Since chest x-rays are readily available and have a low radiation burden, the pattern of nodal and parenchymal involvement is typically used to 'stage' sarcoidosis ( [chest x-ray staging of sarcoidosis](https://radiopaedia.org/articles/thoracic-sarcoidosis-staging?embed_domain=buyreviewer.com%25252525252Fbest-tire-inflator-on-amazon%25252525252F&lang=us)) 3.

Otherwise, the radiographic features are organ-dependent, please see individual articles (listed above).

#### Treatment and prognosis

Treatment is predominantly performed to protect individual organ function and lessen symptom burden, with wise use of corticosteroids and other immunosuppression 13.

Pulmonary involvement is responsible for the majority of morbidity and mortality in patients with sarcoidosis. The overall mortality rate is approximately 5%, with patients who present insidiously faring worse than those who present with an acute onset 1,3. The likelihood of resolution depends on the stage of disease at presentation 3:

- stage I: 60% resolution within 1-2 years

- stage II: 46%

- stage III: 12%

##### Complications

Complications depend on the systemic pattern of disease involvement. As pulmonary sarcoidosis is by far the most common manifestation, so are thoracic complications. They include 3:

- [pulmonary fibrosis](https://radiopaedia.org/articles/pulmonary-fibrosis?embed_domain=buyreviewer.com%25252525252Fbest-tire-inflator-on-amazon%25252525252F&lang=us) ( [stage IV](https://radiopaedia.org/articles/thoracic-sarcoidosis-staging?embed_domain=buyreviewer.com%25252525252Fbest-tire-inflator-on-amazon%25252525252F&lang=us))

- [pulmonary arterial hypertension](https://radiopaedia.org/articles/pulmonary-hypertension-1?embed_domain=buyreviewer.com%25252525252Fbest-tire-inflator-on-amazon%25252525252F&lang=us) and [cor pulmonale](https://radiopaedia.org/articles/cor-pulmonale-2?embed_domain=buyreviewer.com%25252525252Fbest-tire-inflator-on-amazon%25252525252F&lang=us)

- [aspergillomas](https://radiopaedia.org/articles/aspergilloma?embed_domain=buyreviewer.com%25252525252Fbest-tire-inflator-on-amazon%25252525252F&lang=us), which may be complicated by [hemoptysis](https://radiopaedia.org/articles/haemoptysis?embed_domain=buyreviewer.com%25252525252Fbest-tire-inflator-on-amazon%25252525252F&lang=us)

#### History and etymology

Historically, sarcoidosis was also known as Besnier-Boeck-Schaumann disease/syndrome, or various combinations thereof, e.g. Besnier-Boeck disease, Boeck disease, or Schaumann syndrome10.

Sarcoidosis is derived from the Ancient Greek terms, σαρξ (sarc) meaning flesh, ειδος (eidos) meaning form, and σις (-sis), a suffix meaning a process 10,11.
