**Cardiomegaly** is a catch-all term to refer to enlargement of the heart, and should not be confused with causes of [enlargement of the cardiomediastinal outline](https://radiopaedia.org/articles/missing?article%5Btitle%5D=enlargement-of-the-cardiomediastinal-outline&lang=us), or [enlargement of the cardiac silhouette](https://radiopaedia.org/articles/enlargement-of-the-cardiac-silhouette?lang=us).

#### Radiographic features

In most cases, merely 'eye-balling' a [chest x-ray](https://radiopaedia.org/articles/chest-radiograph?lang=us) will be sufficient in detecting cardiomegaly (as the heart is either clearly normal in size or clearly abnormally enlarged). In equivocal cases, the [cardiothoracic ratio](https://radiopaedia.org/articles/cardiothoracic-ratio?lang=us) (CTR) can be easily calculated on an erect PA chest x-ray. The CTR measures the width of the cardiac silhouette and the thoracic cavity;

Traditionally, a ratio greater than 0.5 has been considered to be abnormal 1. However a CTR of >0.55 has been shown to be more specific for cardiac chamber enlargement 1,2.

Specific [cardiac chamber enlargement](https://radiopaedia.org/articles/cardiac-chamber-enlargement?lang=us) can be recognized by cardiac contour changes, new or different interfaces with adjacent lung, and/or displacement of adjacent mediastinal structures. These are discussed separately:

- [right atrial enlargement](https://radiopaedia.org/articles/right-atrial-enlargement?lang=us)

- [right ventricular enlargement](https://radiopaedia.org/articles/right-ventricular-enlargement?lang=us)

- [left atrial enlargement](https://radiopaedia.org/articles/left-atrial-enlargement?lang=us)

- [left ventricular enlargement](https://radiopaedia.org/articles/left-ventricular-enlargement?lang=us)
